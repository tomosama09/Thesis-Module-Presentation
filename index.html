Background of the Study

Vocabulary supports reading and speaking.
Repeated exposure retains new vocabulary (Nation, 2022).

Reading Narrative Texts An effective approach to acquire vocabulary through relatable events, characters, and situations.
The Challenge English exposure in Indonesia is limited (EF EPI 2025). Learners desperately need opportunities to use English in familiar settings.
Non-formal Education An additional learning space
NAC SA3 The New Apostolic Church (NAC) Wedomartani 2, Sekolah Agama 3 (SA3)
Meaningful Context Familiar religious content can become a meaningful context for English learning

PROBLEMS

01

Occasional Usage

English is only occasionally used in certain divine services.

02

Lack of Integration

English is not integrated into children and youth programs.

03

Absent from Curriculum

English is entirely absent from SA3 materials and lesson delivery.

04

No Existing Resources

No structured English learning resource exists for SA3.

Resulting in limited opportunities to practice English in a familiar religious context.

THEORETICAL FRAMEWORKS

Three needs, four frameworks

Each framework addresses a specific instructional need identified in the analysis.

Accessible English texts

Intensive Reading

Short, adapted Bible texts with controlled vocabulary provide comprehensible input for beginner-level readers.

Nation & Macalister, 2021; Hedgcock & Ferris, 2018
Structured vocabulary support

Bilingual Support

Indonesian scaffolding for abstract terms. English stays primary; the L1 clarifies meaning without replacing it.

Cummins, 2008
Scaffolded communicative activities

Communicative Language Teaching

English as meaning-making, not drills.

Brandl, 2021
Meaningful outcomes

Task-Based Learning

Every sub-unit ends in a concrete, communicative outcome, such as a timeline, role-play, plan, or presentation.

(Ellis, 2003)

DESIGN PHASE

From needs to design

Assessment tasks

  • Reading comprehension
  • Grammar practice
  • Task performance
  • Written reflection

  • Learning Activities

From research to reality.

What follows is the module itself.

Illustrations carry the first impression, activities carry the learning.

01 God's Salvation Plan

Students learn English through Old Testament stories about promise, faith, struggle, and hope.

02 Jesus' Teaching

Students learn English through Jesus’ parables about love, forgiveness, responsibility, and good choices.

03 Church History

Students learn English through stories of the first Christians, service, mission, and church growth.

04 Our Church

Students learn English through NAC history, sacraments, ministry, and global church identity.

Vocabulary activities with bilingual support

Schema activation (Hedcock & Ferris, 2018) and background knowledge (Grabe, 2009). Transfers existing knowledge to the second language (Cummins, 2008) and clarifies key vocabulary (Patonah & Irawan, 2022).

Validated by experts

The module was evaluated using a 22-item validation form (4-point scale, 88 points maximum) by two expert validators with complementary expertise.
